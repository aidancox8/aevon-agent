# Founding Client Offer

The play: trade a discounted first build for proof (case study + testimonial + referrals).
Use this for the NEXT warm lead or a network/pilot contact. Do NOT discount Jean unless
you need it to close. He is a real full-price opportunity.

## Why discounted, not free
A client who pays something takes it seriously, does not ghost, and gives a credible
testimonial ("we paid and it was worth it" beats "he did it free"). Free signals zero value.
The discount is framed as a founding rate, not "I am cheap", so it does not anchor your
real pricing low.

## The offer (say it roughly like this)

> I am taking on my first couple of clients at a founding rate. You get the full build at
> 40 to 50 percent off, and in exchange I ask for three things if it works well: a short
> testimonial, permission to write up an anonymous case study, and an introduction to one
> or two people in your network who might want the same. You own the software either way.

## What they get
- The full custom build, scoped to their actual workflow
- Founding rate: 40 to 50 percent off the one-time build
- A locked founding rate on the monthly agent operations tier (see quote template)
- Direct access to the person who built it

## What you get (the whole point)
- A real paying client (validates willingness to pay)
- A written testimonial you can use everywhere
- An anonymous case study ("How a Lower Mainland [X] automated its [Y]")
- 1 to 2 warm referrals into their network
- Real delivery data: what they actually needed, what it took to build, what to charge next

## Guardrails
- Cap it at 2 founding clients, then the rate is gone. Scarcity is real and it protects pricing.
- Still take a deposit before building. Founding rate, not "pay after you are happy."
- Pick a first project you can deliver well and fast. A clean, fast win is worth more than a
  bigger, messier one. Momentum and proof beat revenue on client #1.
- Get the testimonial ask agreed UP FRONT, in writing, as part of the deal. Do not hope for it later.

## Where this fits
This is the parallel path so your first-client outcome does not hinge on Jean alone.
Pursue Jean at full price. Keep this ready for the next warm lead. If Jean closes first,
he is client #1 and you may never need this.
